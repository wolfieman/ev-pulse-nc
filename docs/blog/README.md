# EV Pulse NC — Blog Series

*An independent, multi-part look at where North Carolina should build its EV charging network — and for whom.*

This series extends a November 2025 study I co-authored on optimizing North Carolina's EV charging buildout. Over the months since, I took the analysis further on my own: a five-phase framework that retested the original forecasts against real data and pushed all the way down to the ZIP-code level. The posts translate that work for a general audience; the full methodology and the complete manuscript sit behind them.

**Where to read it:** new posts go live weekly on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/wolfgangsanyer/) and, in expanded form, on Substack. Each post is added to this folder once it has published — so this page fills in as the series runs.

## The series

1. **The Return** — A six-month reckoning. I published forecasts for North Carolina's EV growth, then went back to see how they held up. What I found reframed the whole project.
2. **Testing the Forecast** — What happens when you grade your own predictions against a year of real data — and the misses all lean the same way.
3. **The Gap Within the Gap** — North Carolina's charging divide isn't where you'd look. The sharpest gaps aren't between regions; they're between neighbors.
4. **Where the Chargers Should Go** — $109 million in federal money, one hard question: where first? A single priority score answers it — and the county at #1 isn't the one most people name.
