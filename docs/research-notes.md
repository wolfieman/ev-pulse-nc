# EV Pulse NC: Research Notes

Accumulated narrative and findings for the BIDA 670 research paper.

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-01
**Phase:** 1 - Forecast Validation

---

## Executive Summary (Draft)

This study validates time series forecasts of electric vehicle (EV) registrations
in North Carolina, comparing predictions generated by SAS Model Studio against
actual NCDOT registration data. The validation reveals that while point forecast
accuracy was acceptable (MAPE 4.36%), the models systematically underestimated
EV adoption, with 68.9% of predictions falling below actual values. This finding
suggests that EV adoption in North Carolina is accelerating faster than
historical patterns predicted.

---

## Phase 1: Forecast Validation

### Study Design

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Training Period | September 2018 - June 2025 (82 months) |
| Validation Period | July - October 2025 (4 months) |
| Geographic Scope | 100 North Carolina counties |
| Target Variable | BEV (Battery Electric Vehicle) registrations |
| Validation Type | True out-of-sample holdout |

### Model Selection by SAS Model Studio

SAS Model Studio auto-selected different time series models per county:

| Model Type | Counties | Description |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| ESM (Exponential Smoothing) | 82 | Weighted averages with decay |
| ARIMA | 13 | Autoregressive integrated moving average |
| UCM (Unobserved Components) | 5* | Structural time series |

*Note: Only 4 UCM counties matched in validation (99 counties total matched).

### Key Findings

#### 1. Point Forecast Accuracy

| Metric | Overall | ESM | ARIMA | UCM |
|--------|---------|-----|-------|-----|
| MAPE | 4.36% | 4.16% | 5.43% | 4.87% |
| MAE | 27.10 | 19.10 | 84.83 | 3.48 |
| RMSE | 114.11 | 74.67 | 252.94 | 6.53 |

**Interpretation:** MAPE under 5% is generally considered good forecast accuracy.
ESM models performed best overall. ARIMA's higher MAE/RMSE reflects its use in
larger, more volatile counties (e.g., Mecklenburg, Wake).

#### 2. Systematic Bias (Key Finding)

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| Mean Bias | +18.36 vehicles | Forecasts too LOW |
| Underprediction Rate | 68.9% | Majority of forecasts exceeded by actuals |
| Direction | UNDERPREDICTION | EV adoption outpaced predictions |

**Interpretation:** The positive bias indicates that actual EV registrations
consistently exceeded forecasts. This is not random error but systematic -
the models underestimated the growth trajectory of EV adoption.

#### 3. Confidence Interval Coverage (Critical Finding)

| Model | Observed Coverage | Expected | Gap |
|-------|-------------------|----------|-----|
| ARIMA | 67.3% | 95% | -27.7 pp |
| ESM | 75.3% | 95% | -19.7 pp |
| UCM | 100.0% | 95% | +5.0 pp |

**Interpretation:** The 95% prediction intervals failed to capture 95% of actual
values. This undercoverage is explained by the systematic bias - when the
forecast center is too low, the entire confidence interval shifts downward,
causing actuals to fall above the upper bound.

### Largest Forecast Misses

| County | Model | Predicted | Actual | Error | Notes |
|--------|-------|-----------|--------|-------|-------|
| Mecklenburg | ARIMA | 17,065 | 18,040 | -975 | Charlotte metro, high growth |
| Wake | ESM | 27,166 | 28,098 | -932 | Raleigh metro, high growth |

**Interpretation:** The largest underpredictions occurred in high-growth urban
counties, suggesting that EV adoption acceleration is concentrated in metro areas.

## Interpretation: Why Did Models Underpredict?

### Hypothesis 1: Accelerating EV Adoption

EV adoption appears to be in an acceleration phase, outpacing historical growth
patterns that the models learned. Potential drivers include:

- **Policy factors:** Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) EV tax credits
- **Supply factors:** Increased EV model availability, Tesla price reductions
- **Infrastructure:** Expanding charging network (NEVI program)
- **Consumer factors:** Increasing familiarity and acceptance

### Hypothesis 2: Structural Break

A structural break may have occurred in the EV market dynamics, making
historical patterns less predictive of future behavior. The models, trained
on 2018-2025 data, may not adequately capture post-IRA market dynamics.

### Evidence Supporting These Hypotheses

1. **Urban concentration of errors:** Mecklenburg and Wake (urban, high-income)
   showed the largest underpredictions
2. **Systematic direction:** 68.9% underprediction is not random
3. **CI failure pattern:** Actuals fall ABOVE upper bounds, not below

## Methodology Notes

### What "95% CI Coverage" Means

When a model generates a 95% confidence interval, it claims: "I expect the
true value to fall within this range 95% of the time." Observing only 67-75%
coverage indicates either:

1. **Biased center:** The forecast point is systematically off (our case)
2. **Narrow intervals:** Uncertainty is underestimated
3. **Structural change:** The data-generating process has changed

In our case, the bias explains the coverage gap - intervals are centered
on a point that is too low.

### Validation Integrity

- **No data leakage:** Models were trained through June 2025; validation uses
  July-October 2025 data downloaded separately
- **True out-of-sample:** The actual values were not available when forecasts
  were generated
- **Apples-to-apples:** Same counties, same time periods, same metric (BEV count)

## Research Paper Narrative (Draft Excerpts)

### For Methods Section

> "Forecast validation followed standard time series evaluation practices.
> SAS Model Studio auto-selected time series models were validated against
> true out-of-sample data from July-October 2025. The validation dataset
> was obtained from NCDOT after the forecast generation date, ensuring
> complete temporal separation and eliminating any possibility of data
> leakage."

### For Results Section

> "The forecast models demonstrated moderate point accuracy (MAPE = 4.36%)
> but exhibited systematic underprediction. Actual EV registrations exceeded
> forecasts in 68.9% of observations, with a mean bias of +18.4 vehicles
> per county-month. Consequently, 95% prediction interval coverage was
> only 75.3%, substantially below the nominal 95% level. This pattern was
> most pronounced in high-growth urban counties, where actual adoption
> outpaced predictions by up to 975 vehicles per month (Mecklenburg County,
> October 2025)."

### For Discussion Section

> "The systematic underprediction observed in this validation suggests that
> EV adoption in North Carolina is accelerating faster than historical
> patterns would predict. This finding has practical implications for
> infrastructure planning: stakeholders should consider adding a 4-5%
> buffer to forecast-based allocations to account for faster-than-predicted
> adoption. The finding also raises methodological questions about the
> applicability of traditional time series models during market acceleration
> phases, when historical patterns may underestimate future growth."
