**"Enhanced EV Infrastructure Gap Analysis Through Forecast Validation, Urban ZIP-Level Targeting, Employment Center Demand Assessment, and Dual-Snapshot Infrastructure Growth Tracking in North Carolina"**

*Emphasis:* All four framework priorities integrated, comprehensive methodology, analytical completeness

## Recommendation

**Title #2** ("Dual-Dimension EV Infrastructure Demand Modeling") is the strongest choice because:

- ✅ **Highlights the key innovation:** Residential + Workplace model (most significant extension)
- ✅ **Specific methodology:** CTPP commuting data (demonstrates data sophistication)
- ✅ **Policy relevance:** Workplace charging justifies 30% of NEVI community funding ($7M)
- ✅ **Addresses equity:** Workplace charging serves renters without home charging access
- ✅ **Clear value proposition:** 3× efficiency gain (15 EVs/port vs. 7.5 EVs/port)
- ✅ **Professional length:** Concise but comprehensive

---

## Alternative Recommendations

**Title #4** is the best alternative if your professor values **spatial analytics methodology** - it emphasizes the multi-scale hierarchical approach (county → ZIP → corridor) which is analytically sophisticated.

**Title #3** works if **forecast validation rigor** is your priority - demonstrates scientific method (hypothesis → test → validate → extend).

## Title Selection Criteria

### What Makes a Strong BIDA 670 Title?

1. **Specificity:** Geographic scope, methodology, data sources clearly stated
2. **Innovation:** What analytical extension distinguishes this from standard analyses?
3. **Policy Relevance:** Real-world stakeholder value (NEVI funding, county planners, etc.)
4. **Methodological Rigor:** Statistical techniques, data integration approaches
5. **Scope Balance:** Ambitious enough to demonstrate mastery, focused enough to complete

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- ❌ Too generic: "EV Infrastructure Analysis in North Carolina"
- ❌ Too technical: "ARIMA(p,d,q) Parameter Optimization for Non-Stationary Time Series"
- ❌ Too narrow: "Wake County ZIP Code Infrastructure Gap Assessment"
- ❌ Too broad: "Comprehensive Multi-State EV Planning Framework"
